# Day 2 — Using Generative AI in Data Analytics

## Course

Google Advanced Data Analytics Professional Certificate

---

## Objective

Today's focus was learning how Large Language Models (LLMs) such as Gemini can improve the workflow of a data professional.

Rather than replacing analysts, LLMs can accelerate many stages of a data science project, from planning and coding to communication.

---

## Scenarios

I worked on a fraud detection use case for a fictional bank using a synthetic fraud dataset.

---

## Key Learnings

- Prompt quality directly affects AI output quality.
- AI can accelerate repetitive analytical tasks.
- Human validation remains essential.
- Technical insights must be translated into business language.
- Machine learning is valuable only when connected to real business problems.

---

## Reflection

Today's lesson changed how I think about AI.

Instead of viewing Gemini as a search engine, I started treating it like a collaborative assistant that helps brainstorm ideas, generate code, explain concepts, and improve productivity.

However, I also learned that AI isn't a replacement for critical thinking. Every recommendation, line of code, and insight still needs verification before being trusted.

The future of analytics belongs to professionals who know both data and AI.
